Neurodiversity Awareness & Acceptance

This page is dedicated to neurodiversity. We create awareness, share stories, and talk honestly about how neurodivergence affects children, adults, and families.

Types of Neurodiversity

  • Autism: Different ways of communicating, processing, and experiencing the world.
  • ADHD: Focus, energy, emotional regulation, and creativity.
  • Dyslexia: Reading and writing differences with strong creative thinking.
  • Dyspraxia: Coordination and motor planning differences.
  • Tourette’s: Involuntary movements and sounds.
  • Other profiles: Any brain that works differently from the “standard”.

Impact on Children & Parents

  • Children: Sensory overload, meltdowns, masking, burnout.
  • Parents: Exhaustion, confusion, guilt, and advocacy.
  • Siblings: Feeling protective or unsure how to help.
  • Community: How teachers and friends can support better.
